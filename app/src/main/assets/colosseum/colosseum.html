The Roman symbol of beauty, power, and genius. “The Colosseum”

What is it?

The Colosseum is an circular amphitheater located in the heart of Rome, Italy. Construction of this structure began under the Emperor Vespasian in AD 72 and was completed in AD 80 under his heir Titus. The amphitheater could accommodate over 50,000 spectators and featured a complex system of underground tunnels and chambers. The outer part of the Colosseum stands four stories tall and showcases a series of arches and columns each adorned with statues and other decorations.

Why?

The Colosseum was made to showcase the wealth of the Roman Empire. It served as source of entertainment of for the Roman people. Gladiator fights were the main events shown in the Colosseum.

How was it made?

The Colosseum was mainly made of concrete and used arches and columns to evenly spread out the weight of the structure. It took many skilled workers to complete it.

Why is it special?

The Colosseum represents the skill and power of ancient Rome. It also inspired techniques that we still use to build things today. It always continues to show us the ingenuity of the people who made it.
