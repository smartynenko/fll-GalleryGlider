The Starry Night by Van Gogh

A Unique Masterpiece

"The Starry Night" is a very famous painting by Vincent van Gogh, a great artist. This painting is special because van Gogh usually painted things like flowers and fields, but here, he painted a night scene with hills, a sky full of stars, and big, fiery-looking trees. People remember this painting for its bright blue and yellow colors and the thick, wavy lines.

What Van Gogh Thought

Van Gogh called this painting a "study of night" or a "night effect" in his letters. His brother Theo, who knew a lot about art, wasn't very impressed at first. He thought van Gogh focused too much on the style and not enough on the real feelings of the scene.

How Van Gogh Made It

Van Gogh had been thinking about painting a Starry Night for a long time. It was hard to paint this scene because he liked painting what he saw in front of him, not just from his imagination. He first tried painting a night scene called "Starry Night over the Rhône" outside in the city of Arles, using gas lights to see. But "The Starry Night" we know was mostly made indoors, in a studio.

Where Van Gogh Was

Van Gogh painted "The Starry Night" while he was staying in an asylum near Saint-Rémy after a difficult time in his life. He was encouraged to paint there. He had a view of mountains and a garden, and he used these views, along with memories and imagination, to create the painting.

What Makes It Special

The painting shows Van Gogh's creative style. He was inspired by medieval woodcuts, which have simple shapes and thick lines. The colors he used for the night sky and stars were not just black and white. He saw many colors in the night, like violet, blue, green, and even lemon and pink in the stars.

The Impact of "The Starry Night"

Van Gogh's mix of imagination, memory, and real-life observation, along with his unique style, made "The Starry Night" very popular. He wanted to inspire others with his night scenes. This painting became very important in the art world, influencing many other artists and becoming one of the most famous paintings by van Gogh. This painting is currently located in Museum of Modern Art, New York, United States of America.
